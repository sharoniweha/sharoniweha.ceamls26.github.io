what_i_learned: |
  Today was the mid-summer symposium, and it felt so good for my team and me to show off what we’ve been working on all summer. Getting to present our progress was great, but honestly, sitting back and listening to everyone else's presentations was just as rewarding. Out of all of them, Project 2 and Project 4 really caught my attention. Project 2 is working on training a robotic arm to put Legos together using the YOLO framework, and hearing them break down that process was pretty cool. I can only imagine how tricky the spatial engineering and tracking must be for a robot. Then, Project 4 talked about their progress and how they are using True Positives, True Negatives, False Negatives and False Positives to test their model’s confusion matrix. It was really interesting to see how they manage their error metrics, especially since keeping an eye on pipeline evaluation is something I've been focusing on a lot lately and Dr. Dacon talked about it in a recent workshop. Overall, seeing how the other teams use such different tools to tackle their problems gave me some great inspiration for my own lab work moving forward.
