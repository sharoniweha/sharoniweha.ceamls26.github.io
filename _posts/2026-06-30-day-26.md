what_i_learned: |
  Today I attended the AWS Summit, and it was such an awesome experience. I was able to go to some great workshops and learn a ton of new things. One of the standout sessions I attended was called The Mythos Avalanche Defending Federal Systems at Machine Speed, which was sponsored by Dynatrace. It was a really eye opening breakout session that dove into how Anthropic's Claude Mythos crossed the zero day line back in April, autonomously finding thousands of vulnerabilities and creating working exploits way faster than humans can actually triage them. The speakers, Adeolu Bilewu and Willie Hicks, walked through a federal contractor's view of the situation, making a super interesting argument that runtime observability, rather than just running more scanners, is the real key to defending systems at machine speed.
Outside of the workshops, I spent time exploring the Village area and checking out the stands for different companies like RedHat, OpenAI, and Datadog. It was great getting to talk to people, learn what each company is focused on right now, and check out their tech. The highlight of the expo floor for me was getting to participate in some of the interactive stands, where I actually got some hands-on experience and built my own agent. Overall, it was a packed, incredibly productive day full of great networking and learning!
